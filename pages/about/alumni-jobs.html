What are Scholars' Lab alumni doing now? Our commitment to mentorship includes continuously supporting our students, as they grow and become integral members of different professional communities. An important part of that support includes exposing them to the varied professional roles and paths previous fellows have taken. The information on this page showcases active alumni employment data collected during spring 2026. It represents 95 out of the 122 existing student records since the Praxis and DH fellowship programs started in 2007. While this page highlights the recent employment achiements for only 95 past students, others are still active grads at UVA, we have lost contact with some, and others became full-time parents.

The Scholars' Lab is proud of our students' ability and talent to chart their own paths in unique ways and also recognizes the increasing complexities of the job market today, academic or otherwise, and the urgent need to train graduates in them. We hope that the recent career accomplishments of other UVA alumni can help current graduate students and advisors learn about the many professional options available to them and point to new potential networks for those who need it, which include but are not limited to the professoriate.

Privacy Notice

Please note that this is a highly simplified version of the full Scholars' Lab alumni records. The data created for the job bubbles below aims to protect the privacy of our community members. For that reason, it excludes personal information like name, cohort year, and specific employment location. It also avoids using individual job titles opting instead for condensing them into broader job categories, whenever possible. People with 2 known employment sources are counted twice, once per role, though they are not explicitly highlighted as such in the bubbles visualization.

Job Placement Bubbles

Each of the blue bubbles below represents a job or employment area that one, or more, of our past students holds. They are arranged in alphabetical order. On click, each job area bubble reveals the UVA PhD programs attended by the people now-employed in the selected category. The numbers that show up between parenthesis, next to some graduate program names, indicate how many students per program are employed in the same, or a similar, kind of role.

For example, clicking on the "Consultant" bubble shows "English (2)" and "Sociology." This means that two individuals who have enrolled in the English PhD program and one in Sociology are employed in a consulting role.

The information displayed does not distinguish whether these positions correspond to the first employment opportunity attained before or after graduation, whether it's a second, or third employment experience post-UVA. It also omits the type of industries that employ, since integrating these complex relationships into the current visualization requires further data analysis. The employment areas shown here relate to 16 different types of industries including K-12 and higher education, non-profit, freelance, marketing, tech, and more.

In the context of the previous example, this means that the label "Consultant" inside the bubbles does not clarify whether the person works in the non-profit or tech industry sector. It also excludes ranks, like "Associate" or "Senior," that often accompany more experienced roles.

On the other hand, the connection between degree and profession effectively suggests that the professional writing, research, and managerial skills that come with the experience of completing a PhD are valuable competencies in multiple profesional environments.
